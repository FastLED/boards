What am I looking at?

What is a USB VID:PID?

Every USB device exposes two 16-bit hex identifiers — a Vendor ID (VID) assigned by the USB-IF to the company that made the chip, and a Product ID (PID) the vendor assigns to that specific product. Together they look like 0x303a:0x1001 and uniquely identify a USB peripheral on the bus.

Three things you can look up

  • USB Vendors VID → company — type 303a or espressif.
  • USB Products VID:PID → device — type 303a:1001 or teensy; primary + all alternates are returned.
  • Boards structured metadata — type esp32dev, cortex-m7, wifi, or arduino.

Tips

  • Punctuation and case are ignored — 0x303A:1001, 303a1001, and 303a 1001 all hit the same product.
  • Architecture searches work too — cortex, m7, xtensa, riscv, avr all find matching boards.
  • On any board row, View JSON opens the raw upstream definition; ↗ source jumps to its GitHub home.

Where the data comes from

Vendor IDs come from public USB-IF allocation registries plus the Espressif and Raspberry Pi registries. Boards come from PlatformIO's platform-* repos and Arduino core boards.txt files. Curated overrides (Teensy attribution, etc.) live on this repo's other branch. The combined database is rebuilt and republished by the Build site workflow.
